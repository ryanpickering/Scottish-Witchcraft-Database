King James' Daemonologie and The Scottish Witchcraft Database

A distance reading, comparison, and analysis of early Scottish witchcraft.

Outline

  • Scottish Witchcraft is interesting, significantly different than other kinds. Qualitative research has been done... what can quantitative research give us?
  • List initial ideas of what will be found. I think there will be a light correspondence between types of witchcraft in the database and what James talks about in Demonology.
  • Set up the agenda. First, laying out methodology - different tools being used, different comparisons being made. Then, demonology - first an explanation of it, then initial findings from text analysis. WHICH ARE: what words show up the most, what words are grouped together, initial assumptions from topic modelling. Also do all of this for newes from scotland
  • Then analyzing certain spreadsheets in the database: list what they are, talk about word counts and such. What do types of things showing up point towards that looking at individual cases doesn't show? Refer to methodology of actual witchcraft database here too.
  • Correlate the two things. How are the findings of both related, how do they relate to our overarching understanding of scottish witchcraft - do they show anything new to us? Summarize and conclude.
